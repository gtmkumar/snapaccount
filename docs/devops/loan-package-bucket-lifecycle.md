## Contents

This bucket stores loan application packages generated by LoanService and ReportService:

- Sanction letters
- Executed loan agreements (digitally signed)
- Disbursement advices
- Amortisation schedules
- Loan account statements
- KYC document copies attached to the loan file

Objects are stored under a per-loan-application prefix:

```
loans/{orgId}/{loanApplicationId}/sanction-letter.pdf
loans/{orgId}/{loanApplicationId}/loan-agreement.pdf
loans/{orgId}/{loanApplicationId}/disbursement-advice.pdf
loans/{orgId}/{loanApplicationId}/amortisation-schedule.pdf
```

## Lifecycle Policy

The lifecycle is configured in `infra/setup.sh` and applied via:
```
/tmp/lifecycle-loan-packages.json
```

### Tiers

| Age | Storage Class | Rationale |
|-----|--------------|-----------|
| 0–90 days | STANDARD | Active origination period. Documents may be accessed frequently (amendments, queries, disbursal tracking). |
| 90 days to 7 years | COLDLINE | Loan is closed or dormant. Documents retained for regulatory compliance. Retrieval is infrequent (audit, dispute, RBI inspection). |
| After 7 years (2,557 days) | DELETE | Retention period elapsed — deletion is compliant with RBI and DPDP Act requirements. |

### Why Coldline (not Nearline) after 90 days?

Loan documents after closure are accessed roughly monthly at most (RBI audits, borrower
copy requests). Nearline has a 30-day minimum storage duration and is priced for weekly
access. Coldline (90-day minimum, quarterly access pattern) is more cost-effective for
the expected access pattern and adds only ~$0.02/GB/month at the volumes anticipated in
the pilot phase.

### Why no ARCHIVE tier?

ARCHIVE has a 365-day minimum storage duration and a retrieval SLA measured in hours.
For RBI inspection or borrower dispute resolution, a Coldline retrieval (milliseconds)
is more appropriate than ARCHIVE. The cost savings from ARCHIVE over Coldline are
marginal at the document sizes involved (~200KB–2MB per loan package).

## Retention Period — 7 Years

### Regulatory basis

1. **RBI Master Direction on Digital Lending (2022), Para 9:** Regulated Entities (REs)
   must maintain records of digital lending for at least 8 years. SnapAccount acts as
   a Lending Service Provider (LSP) — the partner bank (RE) is responsible for their
   own 8-year retention. SnapAccount's LSP obligation mirrors the bank's: **7 years**
   from loan closure date is the adopted minimum to align with the Income Tax Act
   (Section 44AA: books of accounts for 6 years) and provide a 1-year buffer.

2. **DPDP Act 2023, Section 8(7):** Personal data must not be retained beyond the
   period necessary for the purpose for which it was collected. Seven years post-closure
   is the defined necessary period for financial records. After 7 years, GCS delete
   lifecycle enforces erasure.

3. **Income Tax Act Section 44AA:** Books of accounts must be maintained for 6 years.
   Loan records are financial records — 7-year retention provides the required 6-year
   window plus a buffer for delayed compliance checks.

### Counting from loan closure vs. origination

The 7-year clock starts from **loan closure date** (final repayment or write-off),
not origination date. The lifecycle rule deletes objects after 2,557 days (~7 years)
from object creation. To ensure correctness:

- LoanService must write loan package objects to GCS only at the time of **loan closure**
  (not at origination). Interim documents (draft sanction letters) must be stored
  separately with a shorter TTL (e.g., in a `temp/` prefix of the documents bucket with a
  30-day delete lifecycle).
- Alternatively, if objects are written at origination, backend-agent must update object
  metadata at closure with a custom metadata field `loan_closed_at` and implement a
  Cloud Function or periodic job to re-apply the lifecycle start date.

**Recommendation (simpler):** Write final executed documents to this bucket only at
loan closure. Keep pre-sanction drafts in the general documents bucket.

## Bucket Lock (Object Lock / Immutable Retention)

GCS supports Bucket Lock (`--retention-period` flag), which makes the lifecycle delete
policy **legally enforceable and irreversible**. Once set, objects cannot be deleted or
overwritten before the retention period expires — even by bucket owners.

**This is NOT enabled by default.**

### When to enable

Enable Bucket Lock only if:
- A regulatory audit requires immutable records (RBI inspection outcome)
- Your legal team advises it for litigation hold

### How to enable (REQUIRES TEAM LEAD APPROVAL)

Enabling Bucket Lock is **permanent and irreversible**. Once locked, the retention period
cannot be reduced. Obtain written approval from the team lead before running:

```bash
# WARNING: IRREVERSIBLE — get approval first
gcloud storage buckets update gs://${GCP_PROJECT_ID}-loan-packages \
    --retention-period=220752000s   # 7 years in seconds (7 * 365.25 * 24 * 3600)
```

Then lock the retention policy (prevents further changes to the period):

```bash
gcloud storage buckets update gs://${GCP_PROJECT_ID}-loan-packages \
    --lock-retention-policy
```

## DPDP Act 2023 — Right to Erasure

Under DPDP Act 2023 Section 12, data principals have the right to request erasure of
their personal data. For loan records, this right is **limited** by the RBI retention
obligation: SnapAccount is legally required to retain records during the 7-year window
and cannot comply with erasure requests that would destroy records required by law.

The lifecycle's delete rule at 2,557 days constitutes the automated erasure mechanism
at retention period expiry. No manual intervention is required.

For borrowers who exercise erasure rights before the 7-year window: respond that retention
is required by law (RBI Master Direction) and document the response.
Backend-agent must implement a `DataErasureRequest` workflow that:
1. Flags the loan record as erasure-requested.
2. Schedules erasure for the loan closure date + 7 years.
3. Generates an automated DPDP compliance response to the borrower.

## Monitoring and Alerting

Recommended Cloud Monitoring alerts (configure manually or via Terraform):

1. **Bucket size alert:** Alert if `storage.googleapis.com/storage/total_bytes` for the
   loan-packages bucket exceeds an expected threshold (indicates runaway write loop).

2. **Object count alert:** Alert if daily object count growth exceeds 10x baseline
   (indicates unbounded object creation bug).

3. **Lifecycle execution metric:** Check `storage.googleapis.com/storage/object_count`
   monthly to confirm lifecycle transitions are occurring as expected.
